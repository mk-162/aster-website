Every club ride has the same three problems. Someone's off the back and nobody's sure how far. Someone's partner is at home wondering whether "back by one" meant anything. And once in a while — rarely, but it's the possibility every ride leader carries — someone comes off on a descent and the group is four kilometres up the road before anyone notices.

One shared map fixes all three. Here's how to set it up for your club, once, in about fifteen minutes.

## The setup.

1. **One person creates the ride** in Aster and shares the join link or QR code to the club group chat. That's the whole admin.
2. **Riders join with a tap** and their phone does the rest. No head unit needed, no fiddling at the café stop — tracking runs in the background, even if the app is closed.
3. **The watch link goes to whoever wants it.** Partners, parents, the members who couldn't make it out. Watching needs no app and no account — it's just a page.

Set the ride to *unlisted* and only people with the link can see it. Your Sunday loop is not broadcast to the world; it's shared with the people you chose.

## What changes on the road.

The ride leader stops doing arithmetic. A glance at the map shows the whole group — who's in the front bunch, who's two minutes back, whether the regroup at the top has actually collected everyone. No more rolling out of the layby while someone is still fixing a puncture out of sight.

And if a rider stops and stays stopped, the map shows it to everyone immediately. There's an SOS button in the app for the worst days — it alerts the ride's contacts with an exact location. It supplements your club's safety habits rather than replacing them, but *exact location, instantly* is a very good thing to have on a lane with no signal bars showing and no street names.

## What changes at home.

The people who love your riders stop waiting. A kitchen-table glance answers the only question they actually have — *are they moving?* — and the finish stops being a guess. More than one club has told us the same small thing: Sunday lunch arguments about lateness simply ended. The map had already said everything.

## What it costs.

Nothing. Club rides on Aster are free — not free-for-now, free because clubs are not who we charge. (Events pay us; you don't.)

Try it on one ride this weekend. If it doesn't make the ride better, it cost you fifteen minutes and one message to the group chat.
